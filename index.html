The Research
🔬
Dehydration & Cognition
A 2011 study in the British Journal of Nutrition found that 1.36% dehydration in women caused degraded mood, increased perception of task difficulty, and reduced concentration — without any physical exercise. Mild hydration loss is invisible until it's already affecting performance.
❤️
Blood Pressure & Stroke Risk
The Framingham Heart Study established that sustained high blood pressure is the single most powerful modifiable risk factor for stroke. A 10 mmHg reduction in systolic pressure reduces stroke risk by approximately 27%. Monitoring matters.
🧪
Cortisol & Memory
Research from Stanford University shows that chronic cortisol exposure reduces dendritic branching in the prefrontal cortex and hippocampus — the regions responsible for memory storage and executive function. Stress isn't just a feeling; it physically reshapes the brain.
📊
Cognitive Load Theory
Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory (1988) established that working memory has a finite capacity of roughly 7±2 elements. Modern research extends this: multitasking reduces task performance by up to 40%, and recovery from cognitive overload takes 20+ minutes.

How BP reveals stress
Under stress the body releases cortisol and adrenaline that constrict blood vessels, raising systolic pressure 10–20 mmHg within minutes. Pulse Pressure reflects arterial stiffness. MAP is average arterial load. RPP measures how hard stress is working your heart.

How Cogniwise tracks each signal
1
Daily 90-second check-in — Sleep quality, energy, perceived stress, and hydration provide baseline cognitive state. These subjective signals are highly predictive of neurological performance when tracked consistently over time.
2
Blood pressure readings — Manual or connected cuff readings are converted into a stress index using Pulse Pressure, Mean Arterial Pressure, and Rate Pressure Product.
3
Google Calendar analysis — Meeting density, back-to-back blocks, and available deep work time are used to estimate cognitive load before your day begins — giving you a predictive view of afternoon fatigue risk.
4
Smart hydration tracking — Water intake is logged via the Cogniwise Smart Bottle or manual input. Time-stamped targets matched to your schedule ensure hydration stays ahead of cognitive impact, not behind it.
5
AI synthesis — All signals are fused into a single CogniScore™ An AI layer translates the combined picture into plain-language insights and prioritised actions — surfacing what matters most, right when you need it.
🔬 The research basis
The Cogniwise scoring model is grounded in published neuroscience. Key sources include: the Framingham Heart Study (BP → stroke risk), Armstrong et al. 2012 in British Journal of Nutrition (dehydration → cognition), McEwen 2007 in Physiology & Behavior (cortisol → hippocampal damage), and Sweller 1988 (Cognitive Load Theory). Cogniwise is a wellness tool, not a medical device.
